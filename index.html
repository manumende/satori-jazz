Japanese 70s avant-garde jazz was a sonic laboratory where a new musical identity emerged. A scene defined by essential albums and rare recordings that explored free improvisation, spirituality, and fusions with local traditions: from daring modalism to new electric forms, and from underground clubs to cult releases for collectors.
